05 What an inquiry call actually is

Since this is your first as owner of Social Bestie — the framing that keeps you from over-selling.

The only 3 goals

  • Qualify fit. Is she a good client for you, and are you a good fit for her? It's a two-way interview.
  • Diagnose. Understand her goals, frustrations, and what "success" means to her — in her words.
  • Book the next step. A paid audit / strategy session / proposal review. The next meeting is the win.

What it is not

  • Not a pitch of your packages or a price quote.
  • Not a monologue about everything you'd do.
  • Not closing the deal on the spot.
  • Not proving you're smart by listing tactics — that's discovery-call territory, later.

The one ratio to remember

Aim to talk ~30%, listen ~70%. The person asking the questions controls the conversation. Every minute she's talking about her business is a minute you're learning how to win it.

06 Your 30-minute game plan

A loose structure, not a script. Time blocks keep you from spending 25 minutes on rapport or diving into tactics too early.

0–4 min

Warm open & rapport

Genuine, specific, brief. Congratulate her on something real, then set the frame for the call.

"Linda, thanks so much for the time. I have to say — I saw you were at the Oscars gifting lounge in Beverly Hills, that's amazing. Before we dive in: my goal today is really just to learn about you and the practice and see if we're a fit. Sound good?"

4–8 min

Frame the call & her time

Set expectations so she relaxes: no hard sell today. Confirm what she hoped to get out of it.

"So there's no pressure here — I won't pitch you a package today. I just want to understand your goals, and if it makes sense we'll book a deeper strategy session. What made you want to hop on a call?"

8–20 min

Discovery — the heart of the call

This is 40% of your time on purpose. Work through the discovery questions (next section). Take notes, reflect back what you hear, dig one layer deeper on each answer.

20–25 min

Reflect back + one insight

Summarize her goal in her words, then offer one sharp, specific observation about her social — proof you did the homework — without giving away the full plan.

"So it sounds like the real goal is filling the membership, not just growing followers. One thing I noticed — your training side barely shows up on the grid, and that's a whole audience of injectors you could be speaking to. That's the kind of thing we'd map out together."

25–30 min

Close to the next step

Don't leave it vague. Propose a concrete next meeting and put it on the calendar before you hang up.

"I'd love to do a proper audit of your accounts and put together a little strategy — walk you through exactly what I'd focus on first. Could we grab 45 minutes next week for that? … Great, does Tuesday or Thursday work better?"

07 Discovery questions to have ready

Pick 6–8, don't machine-gun all of them. Open-ended, then follow her thread. The bolded ones are your must-asks.

Goals & vision

  1. "What does growing the business look like for you over the next year — more patients, more members, the training side, something else?"
  2. "If we nailed the social side, what would actually change for the practice?"
  3. "Which is the priority right now — the med spa, or the injector training business?"

Current state & pain

  1. "How are you handling social right now — is it all you, or do you have help?"
  2. "What's the most frustrating part of it? What makes you want to hand it off?"
  3. "When you get busy with patients, what happens to the content?"
  4. "Are the two accounts — your personal page and the practice page — working together or kind of doing their own thing?"

Results & measurement

  1. "How do new patients find you today — referrals, Instagram, Google, walk-ins?"
  2. "Do you know if Instagram is actually turning into bookings, or is it hard to tell?"
  3. "How full are the memberships — Botox Club, Beauty Vault? Is filling those a goal?"

Fit & logistics

  1. "Have you worked with a social agency or freelancer before? How did that go?"
  2. "What would make this a no-brainer 'yes' for you?"
  3. "Who else is involved in a decision like this — is Dr. Rank part of it?"
  4. "What's your timeline — is this a now thing or a next-few-months thing?"

08 Likely objections & calm responses

She's a savvy buyer who's probably been pitched before. Don't argue — acknowledge, reframe, redirect to the next step. Never wing the price on an inquiry call.

She might say…Your calm reframe
"I already do my own social and it's doing fine." "Totally — and honestly it shows, your page is one of the better ones I've seen in the space. The question I'd explore is whether it's turning into booked members, and what it's costing you in time you'd rather spend injecting."
"What do you charge?" "Great question — it depends on scope, so I'd hate to throw out a random number. Let me do a proper audit first, then I can show you a couple of options that actually fit your goals. That's what I'd cover in our next call."
"I've been burned by an agency before." "I hear that a lot, and it's fair. What went wrong last time? … That's exactly the stuff I'd want to do differently — mind if I ask what 'good' would've looked like?"
"How is this different from all the other people pitching me?" "Honestly, most will promise followers. I care about members and consults booked — and I actually looked at how your training audience is underserved. That's a lever most people wouldn't even notice."
"I don't really have time for this right now." "That's the exact reason to explore it — the whole point is to take it off your plate. No commitment today; let's just do the audit and you can decide from there."
