01 The 60-second snapshot

If you remember five things walking in, remember these.

  • Her audience follows her, not the job. Every time she shows up as a full person — bride, runner, someone with ADHD, someone healing her skin — she 3–4x's her ceiling. That's the whole strategy in one line.
  • One personal brand, two tones. Instagram = "Chelsea the injector" (comedy). TikTok = "Chelsea the whole person" (carousels, vulnerable). Not business vs. personal — same brand, two voices.
  • She's not broken, she's un-systemized. She's been doing the hard part (creating) with no container. Don't hand her a strategy essay — hand her a rhythm. Simplicity is the product.
  • Design around the constraints, don't fight them. ADHD → fewer decisions. Busy → batchable. Camera-shy → formats that need zero glam. Every recommendation respects all three.
  • The camera roll is the goldmine. Her best-ever format (wedding carousels) reuses photos she already has. Find out how many are still unposted — that's months of proven content sitting on her phone.

05 Chelsea's content operating system

This is the thing to walk her through and hand her at the end of the call. It's not an app and it's not a training program — it's a simple weekly rhythm plus one batch day a month. That's the whole "system."

The weekly rhythm — fixed content types, not fixed topics

Fixing the format per platform kills decision fatigue (the ADHD tax). She's never staring at a blank calendar wondering what to make — the format is already decided, she just picks the pillar.

PlatformDefault formatPull from these pillars
TikTok Carousels (static photos, no filming) Wedding · Burnout/ADHD · Running · Skin journey
Instagram Comedy skits + 1 crossover carousel / week Injector personality + one personal-life carousel

One batch day a month

  • Film 8–10 skits in 2–3 outfits in a single sitting → weeks of IG covered
  • Separately, pull photos for 2–3 carousels
  • Solves consistency and "I'm not camera-ready" in one move — she's only on camera when she's already glam

Lowest-friction formats to lean on

  • Carousels — static photos, no filming at all
  • Prop / costume skits — the trunk or syringe gag she already wins with
  • Voiceover-over-text — no live talking-head needed

How to hand it over

Don't present it as strategy — present it as relief. "You don't decide what to post anymore. TikTok day = a carousel. Instagram = a skit. Once a month we batch, and you're covered. That's it."

06 The call flow

A loose structure, not a script. Trust → validation → plan → one commitment. Keep the plan simple out loud; every time you're tempted to add nuance, cut instead.

0–5 min

Open with her win

Lead with the numbers, not advice. Make her feel seen by her own data first.

"Your two best posts ever are the wedding carousels — 16.9 and 16.5×. Your burnout post beat your best injector joke by more than 3×. That gap is the whole conversation today."

5–10 min

Validate the pain points

Name each one as normal, not a flaw, before you solve it.

"Consistency is a systems problem, not a discipline problem — especially on 12-hour shifts. And we're going to build this around the ADHD, not pretend it's not there. Fewer decisions, not more."

10–20 min

Reframe the split + present the 4 pillars

One brand, two tones. Then the pillars as her menu — she picks, never invents.

20–35 min

Walk her through the operating system

The weekly rhythm + monthly batch day. Keep it to one screen. Sell it as relief, not strategy.

35–45 min

Ask the money question, then close on ONE action

Get the camera-roll answer. Then pick the single easiest win and book the batch day before you hang up.

"This week: post Wedding Details Part 3 — you probably already have the photos. And let's put your batch day on the calendar right now."

07 Questions to have ready

Pick a handful, follow her thread. The bolded one is the must-ask of the whole call.

The money question

  1. "How much unposted wedding content and how many photos are actually sitting on your phone right now?" — this is her single biggest proven opportunity. Quantify it live; don't let the call end without an answer.

Capacity & rhythm

  1. "When a busy stretch of shifts hits, what's the first thing that falls off? Filming, or posting what you've already got?"
  2. "If you never had to decide what to post again — just the format was picked for you — would that feel like relief or like a cage?"
  3. "Where do you actually film best — home, the medspa, on a run?"

The untapped pillar

  1. "How do you feel about putting running on the account? It's your passion and literally no other injector owns it."
  2. "What's the honest relationship between running and the ADHD / the shifts? That tension is the content."

08 If she pushes back

Acknowledge, reframe, redirect — never argue. She knows her audience; you know the pattern in the data.

She might say…Your reframe
"Running feels off-brand / random." "It's the opposite — it's your differentiator. Nobody in your injector niche owns running. It's how you stop being 'an injector account' and become Chelsea's account. Plus it's built-in ADHD and stress-management content."
"I don't have time to batch a whole day." "That's exactly why we batch. One focused sitting replaces the daily 'ugh what do I post' that's actually eating your time. It's less total time, just front-loaded."
"Carousels feel low-effort / lazy." "They're your single best-performing format. Your audience and the algorithm disagree with the 'lazy' feeling. Low effort for you plus high performance is literally the goal."
"I don't always look cute enough to post." "Then you never have to be on camera to post. Carousels and voiceover-over-text carry the whole week. Camera only happens on batch day, when you're already ready."

10 Do & don't

Do
  • Lead with the data, then feelings, then plan.
  • Validate ADHD / inconsistency / camera fatigue as normal.
  • Keep the plan to one screen: 1 calendar, 4 pillars, 1 batch day.
  • Get the camera-roll number.
  • Leave with one booked action (Part 3 + batch day).
Don't
  • Don't hand her a complex strategy doc.
  • Don't add nuance when in doubt — cut it.
  • Don't imply her current content is bad — it's un-systemized.
  • Don't assign four next steps. Assign one.
  • Don't let it end without the camera-roll answer.
