# Chelsea Content Strategy — Call Prep & Run Sheet

**Goal of the call:** Get Chelsea to leave with (1) belief that the data has her answer, (2) a dead-simple system she can actually stick to with ADHD + a busy schedule, and (3) one clear next action. Do NOT hand her a strategy essay. Simplicity is the product.

**Her real constraints (design around these, don't fight them):** ADHD → low decision-friction. Busy → batchable. Camera fatigue → formats that don't need glam. Everything below is built to respect all three.

---

## The one-sentence thesis (say this out loud early)
> "Your audience follows *you*, not the injector job — and the data proves it. Every time you show up as a full person, you 3–4x your ceiling. So the whole plan is just: make it easy to keep showing up as you."

---

## Call flow (~45–60 min)

### 1. Open with the win (5 min) — build trust before advice
Lead with numbers, not opinions. She needs to feel *seen* by her own data.
- "Your two best-performing posts ever are the wedding carousels — **16.9x and 16.5x**. That's not a fluke, that's a signal."
- "Your career/burnout 'walking away from typical nursing' post hit **8.7x**. Your best pure injector-joke post hit **2.6x**. That gap is the whole strategy."
- Land it: **the vulnerable, whole-person content is 3–4x the ceiling of injector-with-jokes.**

### 2. Validate the pain points (5 min) — normalize before you solve
Say each one back to her as normal, not as a flaw:
- **Consistency:** "Consistency is the #1 thing every creator struggles with, especially on a 12-hour-shift schedule. It's a systems problem, not a discipline problem."
- **ADHD:** "We're going to build this *around* the ADHD, not pretend it's not there. The plan has fewer decisions, not more."
- **Camera confidence:** "You should never have to be full-glam to post. Your best format literally uses photos you already have."
- **'Random videos':** "You've been doing the hard part — creating. We're just adding a container so it compounds."

### 3. Reframe the platform split (5 min)
Not business vs. personal — it's two *tones* of one brand:
- **Instagram = "Chelsea the injector"** → comedy-forward, skits, tied to the job.
- **TikTok = "Chelsea the whole person"** → carousels, vulnerable, personal.
- The big unlock: **the winning TikTok formats have barely been tried on Instagram.** Cross-pollinate.

### 4. Present the 4 pillars (10 min) — this is the spine
1. **Wedding story series (carousels)** — proven #1. Zero filming. Reuses existing photos.
2. **Injector personality / comedy skits** — proven Instagram winner (trunk gag, syringe prop, etc.).
3. **Running + nurse-life balance** — *untapped*, matches her real passion, differentiator no other injector is doing.
4. **Vulnerability / relatable struggle** (burnout, ADHD, skin journey) — proven TikTok winner, barely tested on IG.

### 5. Present the system (10 min) — keep it to ONE screen
Fixed content *types* per day, NOT fixed topics (topics = decisions = friction):

| | TikTok | Instagram |
|---|---|---|
| **Format** | Carousels | Comedy skits + occasional crossover carousel |
| **Pillars** | Wedding · Burnout/ADHD · Running · Skin journey | Injector personality + 1 personal carousel/wk |

- **One batch day per month:** film 8–10 skits in 2–3 outfits in a single sitting → weeks of IG covered. Separately, pull photos for 2–3 carousels. This solves consistency AND camera-readiness in one move.
- **Prioritize the lowest-friction formats:** carousels (no filming) · prop/costume skits (trunk, syringe) · voiceover-over-text (no talking head).

### 6. The single most important question (5 min)
> **"How much unposted wedding content and how many photos do you actually have sitting on your phone right now?"**

This is her biggest proven opportunity. If she has a camera roll full of wedding photos, she's sitting on months of 16x content. Quantify it live.

### 7. Close with ONE next action (5 min)
Don't assign four things. Pick the single easiest win:
- **This week:** post "Wedding details I'm so glad we chose, Part 3" (carousel — she likely already has the photos).
- **Book the batch day** on the calendar before you hang up.

## 5 ready-to-pitch content ideas (have these in your back pocket)
1. **"Wedding details I'm so glad we chose, Part 3"** — TikTok carousel (proven format, ready now)
2. **"What being a nurse taught me about planning my own wedding"** — IG carousel (cross-pollinated format)
3. **"POV: married to an aesthetic nurse who 'just wants to try something' on your face"** — IG comedy skit
4. **"Why I almost quit nursing and what running taught me instead"** — TikTok vlog/carousel (vulnerability + running)
5. **"Training for a half marathon on 12-hour nurse shifts"** — TikTok day-in-the-life (running pillar)

## Objection handling (if she pushes back)
- **"Running feels off-brand / random."** → "It's the opposite — it's the differentiator. Nobody in your injector niche owns running. It's how you stop being 'an injector account' and become *Chelsea's* account. And it's built-in ADHD/stress-management content."
- **"I don't have time to batch a whole day."** → "That's exactly why we batch — one focused sitting replaces the daily 'ugh what do I post' decision that's actually eating your time. It's less total time, front-loaded."
- **"Carousels feel low-effort / lazy."** → "They're your single best-performing format. The algorithm and your audience disagree with the 'lazy' feeling. Low effort *for you* + high performance is the entire goal."
- **"I don't always look cute enough to post."** → "Then you never have to be on camera to post. Carousels and voiceover-over-text carry the whole week. Camera days only happen on the batch day when you're already ready."

---

## Facilitation reminders
- **Lead with data, then feelings, then plan.** Trust → validation → system.
- **Fewer decisions win.** Every time you're tempted to add nuance, cut it instead.
- **Get one concrete commitment** (the Part 3 carousel + a booked batch day) before the call ends.
- **The camera roll question is the money question** — don't let the call end without an answer.
